World — Dickens, Paradox & History

A short article outlining the famous opening of A Tale of Two Cities.

Quote

“It was the best of times, it was the worst of times…”

Meaning & context

Charles Dickens's sentence juxtaposes opposites to capture the contradictions of the late-18th-century world — both the hope of revolutionary change and the terror that accompanied it. Critics note its role as an antithetical framing device and a map of major themes: duality, resurrection, social conflict.

Quick notes

  • Antithesis sets the tonal contrast.
  • Prepares reader for extremes and social upheaval.
