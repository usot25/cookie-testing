Politics — Austen's Irony

The politics of marriage and property in Regency England.

Quote

“It is a truth universally acknowledged, that a single man in possession of a good fortune, must be in want of a wife.”

Meaning & context

Austen's opening line uses irony to critique social assumptions about marriage, wealth, and status. The narrator's faux-maxim highlights how marriages were treated as economic transactions more than romantic unions.

Quick notes

  • Introduces narrator's ironic tone.
  • Frames marriage and social status as central themes.
