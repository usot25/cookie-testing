Culture — Tolkien's Poetic Lines

Finding meaning in short poems and songs.

Quote

“Not all those who wander are lost.”

Meaning & context

This line suggests that wandering may be purposeful. In Tolkien's context it refers to characters whose outward presentation of wandering hides strength and intent — often quoted in travel and personal-growth contexts.

Quick notes

  • From a poem included in The Lord of the Rings.
  • Often used as a motto for exploration and discovery.
